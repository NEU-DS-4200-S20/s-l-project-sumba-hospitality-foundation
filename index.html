Itamar Zik, Luke Janik, Sang Min Joung, Orson Spence

Service-Learning Course Project as part of DS 4200: Information Presentation and Visualization, taught by Prof. Dylan Cashman, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Motivation

The purpose of our design is to tell the story of Sumba Hospitality Foundation (SHF). We chose to take a visual storytelling approach which focuses on the geographic significance of the Island of Sumba. As a short background, SHF is an eco-hotel that attracts visitors from around the world, but is also a school that teaches local islanders hospitality and agricultural techniques and helps them find jobs after graduation. SHF expressed that it can be difficult to describe their model to potential guests and investors, and wanted us to try to tackle the problem using interactive visualizations. Because there are a series of steps in Sumba’s process, we felt that the most effective way to describe it to a user is through a collection of visualizations that are ordered “chronologically” and offer transitions between each visualization.

Data Analysis

Most of our data is categorical, but we do have some quantitative attributes like income and family size. Our main goal with this particular dataset is twofold. First, we want to use the categorical data about family size and parents occupation to understand the demographics of SHF’s student body. Second, we want to use the information about current income and the amount of money that students return to their families to demonstrate the impact that SHF has had on the lives of its students and their families. The data was collected by survey, which was sent to all the existing alumni of SHF. Below are some of the questions we asked, and how we processed this data:

- Do you help parents earn money or work to support them?

categorical data to determine which alumni we can include in our (Where does the money go?) visualization

- Are you currently working full time?

categorical data to include in our interactive visualization, to show the network of Sumba alumni around the world

- What is your current income?

categorical data to include in our interactive visualization, using a gradient to show income levels for alumni

- How many family members do you support with that money? For what purpose do they spend the money from you?

quantitative data to determine the categories for our (Where does the money go?) visualization

Task Analysis

Because the goal of our project was to describe the model of SHF, we had a wide variety of tasks. The first aspect of the foundation that we wanted to capture in our project was the perspective of the guests. We wanted to be able to identify the nationality of each guest to give an idea of where the guests at SHF were from, and describe how they rated their stay at the resort. This data would be presented in a way that would allow the user to browse positive reviews and the variety of countries that guests come from. Next, we wanted to provide more information about the school and the island of Sumba to contextualize the impact that SHF has on its students’ lives. Here we would give the user an opportunity to explore the data about SHF and learn about its student body and sustainability. To complete the impact map, we wanted to show how SHF alumni increase their income by working at luxe resorts in Southeast Asia, and how they can use their increased salaries to support family back home. This would also be accomplished by allowing the user to explore data about where the students work post-graduation as well as how much money they send home.

Design Process

Conclusion

Right now, we have the foundation for a majority of our visualizations completed and have written the code required for transitioning between each stage in our visualization. There are two animated visualizations, a Sankey diagram and a world map, that are incomplete because of confusion around using animated particles in d3, we will be diving into this in the following weeks to complete this with full functionality. Additionally, we would like the styling to be consistent across all our visualizations and will add paragraphs in-between certain stages to help contextualize the visualization and give more information about SHF. We are looking to have this visualization page tell a story that is - the way in which Sumba Alumni go on to contribute to the world. This week we are looking to add detail to our visualizations, so we will be coloring the maps based on guest and alumni data using a color gradient to encode the quantity. As well as create a continuous scrolling feature between our visualizations so that it is one continuous story. Finally, we would also like to use the template from the guest data map to render the 2nd map that shows the global alumni network, and includes functionality that allows for visitors to see some of the individual alumni and their stories, such as their aspirations, and current job (this is data we collected in our survey.
