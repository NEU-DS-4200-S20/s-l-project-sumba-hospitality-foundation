Comparing GDP
Gross domestic product (GDP) is a measure of the market value of goods and services produced by a region in a year.

This graph shows the GDP per capita at purchasing power parity (PPP) which is useful when comparing living standards between nations.
Jakarta's GDP PPP is $57,906
Jakarta is Indonesia's largest city and is the capital of the country. It boasts a booming economy supported by finance and manufacturing.
Indonesia as a whole
While the city thrives, its GDP is over four times the national average. The country's average GDP is $13,320.
Sumba
Sumba is an island in the West Nusa Tenggara region. It's GDP PPP is $5,761, less than half the country's average. Moreover, this is adjusted for cost of living. The per capita GDP is $1,650.
